# 📱 Social Media Responsibility Guide for Students

## ✅ Good Practices
- Keep your online presence clean, respectful, and professional  
- Communicate politely, even when you disagree with someone  
- Protect your accounts using strong passwords and security features  
- Share content that is accurate, helpful, and meaningful  
- Be mindful of how much time you spend scrolling  

---

## 🚫 Things to Avoid
- Avoid posting anything that could negatively affect your future opportunities  
- Do not share sensitive personal information (like address, phone number, or passwords)  
- Don’t trust or forward information without verifying its authenticity  
- Stay away from arguments, trolling, or harmful online behavior  
- Don’t let social media usage affect your mental health or productivity  

---

## 📊 Quick Checklist

| Positive Habits | Risky Behaviors |
|----------------|----------------|
| Stay respectful in interactions | Post offensive or harmful content |
| Use strong security measures | Share confidential details |
| Fact-check before sharing | Spread misinformation |
| Manage screen time | Waste excessive time online |
| Maintain a positive image | Engage in toxic discussions |

---

## 🧠 Final Thought
Using social media responsibly helps you build a strong digital image while keeping your personal and professional life safe.
