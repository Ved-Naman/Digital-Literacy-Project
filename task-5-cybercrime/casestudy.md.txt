# 🛡️ Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud (QR Code Scam)

## 1. Introduction
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where individuals are tricked into transferring money through digital payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or BHIM. These scams mainly rely on social engineering, exploiting users’ lack of awareness rather than technical hacking.

---

## 2. How the Fraud Happens
A common scenario involves online buying and selling platforms. A student lists an item for sale and is contacted by a fraudster pretending to be a genuine buyer.

- The fraudster quickly agrees to pay the full amount to gain trust.
- Instead of asking for the seller’s UPI ID, they send a QR code via WhatsApp.
- The victim is told to scan the code to receive payment.
- The victim scans the code and enters their UPI PIN.
- In reality, this action authorizes a payment, and money is deducted from their account.

---

## 3. Targeted Victims
UPI fraudsters commonly target:
- Students and young users
- Online sellers on platforms like OLX
- Individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems

---

## 4. Consequences
The consequences of such fraud include:
- Immediate financial loss
- Emotional stress and panic
- Difficulty in recovering the money

---

## 5. Conclusion
UPI fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness. Users must understand that entering a UPI PIN always authorizes a payment, not receipt. Being cautious and verifying transactions can help prevent such scams.
